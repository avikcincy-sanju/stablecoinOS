01

Which stablecoin collapsed in 2022, wiping out $40 billion in a single week?

TerraUSD (UST) and its sister token LUNA collapsed in May 2022. The algorithmic stablecoin relied on a mint/burn mechanism with LUNA to maintain its peg — a design that failed catastrophically when confidence evaporated, triggering a death spiral. It remains the largest stablecoin failure in history.

02

How much cheaper is a stablecoin cross-border payment vs. a SWIFT wire?

According to a Federal Reserve March 2026 note, stablecoin transactions cost $0.01–$1.00 with sub-minute settlement, versus $25–$50 per SWIFT wire with 1–5 day settlement. That's a 100x to 1,000x cost reduction on international corridors.
↓ Tap to reveal
03

What does the GENIUS Act stand for, and when was it signed into law?

The GENIUS Act — Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins — was signed into law by President Trump in July 2025. It created the first federal regulatory framework for payment stablecoins, with full implementation targeted by July 2026.
↓ Tap to reveal
04

How much annual transaction volume did stablecoins process in 2025?

Stablecoins processed approximately $46 trillion in transaction volume in 2025 — that's more than 20x PayPal, nearly 3x Visa, and rapidly approaching the ACH network's total volume. This marked a new all-time high.
↓ Tap to reveal
05

What is the x402 protocol and why does it matter for AI?

x402 is an HTTP payment primitive that revives the original HTTP 402 "Payment Required" status code. It enables AI agents, software, and APIs to pay each other in stablecoins — instantly, permissionlessly, without invoicing or banks. It transforms payment from an operational layer into a network behavior.
↓ Tap to reveal
06

Which blockchain carries the most USDT volume — Ethereum or Tron?

Tron dominates USDT transaction volume, especially in emerging markets. While Ethereum holds more USDT by market cap, Tron's lower fees make it the dominant rail for actual transfers — particularly for P2P remittances in Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America.
↓ Tap to reveal
07

What is MiCA and which bloc passed it into law?

MiCA — Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation — is the European Union's comprehensive digital asset regulatory framework. It came into full force in December 2024. For stablecoins (e-money tokens), it requires 1:1 reserve backing, licensing with a national competent authority, and strict governance standards.
↓ Tap to reveal
08

What was the stablecoin total market cap at the end of Q1 2026?

The stablecoin market cap reached a record $315–321 billion at the end of Q1 2026. Notably, this growth occurred while the broader cryptocurrency market fell over 20%, demonstrating stablecoins' role as a defensive asset class and genuine payment infrastructure.

Stablecoin Timeline

FROM BITFINEX
TO THE WORLD

2014

Tether (USDT) Launches

The first major fiat-backed stablecoin goes live, issued by Tether Ltd on the Bitcoin blockchain via Omni Layer. The concept of a "stable" crypto asset is born.

2018

USDC & the Regulated Era

Circle and Coinbase launch USDC under the Centre Consortium — the first regulated, audited stablecoin. This marks the shift toward institutional-grade assets.

2019

Facebook's Libra Shakes Regulators

Meta's attempt at a global stablecoin triggers congressional hearings worldwide and accelerates regulatory thinking on what stablecoins mean for sovereignty.

2021

DeFi Summer & the Stablecoin Explosion

DAI, UST, and dozens of yield-bearing stablecoins attract hundreds of billions into DeFi protocols. The programmable money thesis gains real traction.

2022

The Terra/LUNA Collapse

UST's algorithmic peg fails, wiping $40B in days. The event reshapes risk understanding and accelerates demand for reserve-backed, regulated stablecoins.

2024

MiCA Goes Live in Europe

The EU's comprehensive crypto regulation creates a licensed pathway for stablecoin issuers — setting a global regulatory template.

2025

GENIUS Act Signed · x402 Emerges

The US gets its first stablecoin law. Simultaneously, x402 emerges as the primitive enabling machine-to-machine stablecoin payments for AI agents.

2026

Stablecoins Become Infrastructure

$321B market cap. $46T annual volume. Visa, JPMorgan, PayPal all building on stablecoin rails. The question is no longer if — but how deep.

Replacing $50 SWIFT wires with sub-dollar transfers — in seconds, not days.

Recipient converts USDT to local currency via a licensed offramp partner. Total cost: under 1%. Total time: under 2 minutes. The SDG 3% target is already beaten.

The Road Ahead

WHAT COMES
NEXT

The forces shaping the next chapter of stablecoin evolution — regulation, technology, and the emerging financial internet.

Near-Term · 2026

GENIUS Act Full Implementation

July 2026 marks the US stablecoin regulation full rollout deadline. Expect a surge in bank-issued stablecoins, stricter reserve attestation, and the first wave of federally licensed payment stablecoin issuers. This could unlock $100B+ in new institutional issuance.

📡 Signal: FDIC rulemaking published · Bank subsidiaries preparing applications
Near-Term · 2026

Yield-Bearing Stablecoins Go Mainstream

Yield-bearing products grew 22% in Q1 2026 and now represent a structural market feature. As interest rates remain elevated, demand for T-bill-backed yield stablecoins — USDY, sUSDS, USDe — will accelerate. The question shifts from "why hold zero-yield" to "which yield product is safest."

📡 Signal: $4.3B added in yield stablecoin cap in Q1 2026 alone
Mid-Term · 2027–2028

Multi-Chain Interoperability Becomes Table Stakes

CCIP, LayerZero, and Wormhole are building the interoperability stack that lets stablecoins flow frictionlessly across chains. By 2027, chain-specific stablecoin silos will be an anachronism — value will route to the cheapest, fastest chain automatically. This is the TCP/IP moment for stablecoin settlement.

📡 Signal: Chainlink CCIP volume growing at 1,972% YoY
Mid-Term · 2027–2028

AI × Stablecoin Economy Emerges

As AI agents proliferate and autonomously execute complex workflows, stablecoin micropayments become their economic operating system. x402 will standardize machine payments. By 2028, a significant portion of on-chain stablecoin volume could be agent-to-agent — invisible to humans, running the global economy's back office.

📡 Signal: a16z names x402 a defining primitive of 2026
Long-Term · 2029+

The Dollar's Digital Dominance

USD-denominated stablecoins processing $46T annually already rival traditional rails. If GENIUS Act regulations attract foreign capital and further global alignment occurs, dollar stablecoins could entrench USD dominance in global digital commerce more firmly than SWIFT ever did — a geopolitical outcome as much as a financial one.

📡 Signal: 99%+ of stablecoin volume is USD-denominated · foreign challengers lagging
Long-Term · 2029+

Private Stablecoins vs. CBDCs

The CBDC vs. stablecoin tension will define monetary policy debates of the late 2020s. Will governments issue digital dollars directly or cede the layer to regulated private issuers? The answer differs by jurisdiction — but private stablecoins have a multi-year head start and network effects that may prove insurmountable.

📡 Signal: Fed CBDC delayed · EU retail CBDC stalled · private stablecoins winning
